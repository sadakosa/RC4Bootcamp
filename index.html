About Curriculum

Strategy

Key question: How to develop a business idea and direction that is successful? This segment will guide participants through the first important steps of conceptualising a startup strategy: from choosing an industry, generating a business idea, setting a direction, planning for feasibility to obtaining validation. Participants will sit in to an emerging industries panel discussion, attend a Stamford-designed workshop on Design Thinking and learn how to use the Business Model Canvas, among other activities.

Marketing

A successful business must create and exchange goods of value with others. How can we accelerate this exchange given the paradigm shift in marketing from the introduction of digital tools? This section will equip participants with the skills to design a holistic marketing strategy and the grasp of cutting-edge marketing tools. These tools are specially curated for start-ups to maximize capital and returns on investment.

- Manager, CEO

Jenny is a Year 3 NUS Business (Accountancy) student who is currently having a whale of a time on exchange in Netherlands (while still dedicating her 100% to organising this event). She has prior work experience with various startups: Cabin (a corporate accounting services firm), Grain (a foodtech delivery company), and Positive Intentions (a social development and training business), hence is more familiar with the Singapore startup scene than most. She has also previously helped organise an international convention on social entrepreneurship with her alma mater, Raffles Girls’ School (Secondary), giving her the experience and expertise to organise an open event of this scale.
